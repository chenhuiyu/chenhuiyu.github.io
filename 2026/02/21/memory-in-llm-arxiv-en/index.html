Mine and Refine: Optimizing Graded Relevance in E-commerce Search Retrieval

Created2026-02-21|Updated2026-02-21|NLP Insights
|Post Views:

Paper Info

  • Chinese title: 《Mine and Refine：优化电商搜索中的分级相关性检索》
  • arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17654v1
  • Paper ID: 2602.17654v1

Summary

This paper proposes a two-stage retrieval training pipeline, Mine and Refine, for semantic embedding retrieval in e-commerce search. Instead of treating relevance as a strict binary label, the authors model graded relevance (exact match / substitutes / complements) and enforce clear score separation across levels. The approach first builds a robust global semantic space, then mines hard examples and refines decision boundaries for better long-tail and noisy-query robustness.

Core Innovations

  1. Graded relevance modeling for realistic search behavior.
  2. Two-stage training: global semantic shaping, then boundary refinement with hard negatives.
  3. Policy-aligned scalable supervision via lightweight LLM + human guideline + behavior auditing.
  4. Robustness enhancements with spelling-noise augmentation and synthetic queries.

Experimental Findings

  • Better retrieval relevance offline, especially on hard/long-tail queries.
  • Statistically significant gains in online A/B tests.
  • Better score calibration for hybrid ranking/thresholding in production systems.

Cipher’s Take

The most valuable idea is not a specific loss function, but turning business-level graded relevance into trainable objectives. For LLM-era retrieval systems, this is highly practical: measurable, iterative, and deployable.
